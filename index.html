Pudina Buttermilk (Mint-Infused Yogurt Lipid Emulsion)

An analysis of endothermic cooling dynamics. This protocol utilizes the volatile, organic essential oils found in fresh mint leaves, bound within a fermented dairy emulsion, to induce an immediate systemic thermodynamic cooling effect.

Synthesis Time 10 Mins
Reaction Temp 274K (Chilled)
Batch Yield 2 Volumetric Glasses

🧪 Essential Reagents

🧬 Protocol Execution

  1. Cellular Dissolution: Introduce fresh mint leaves and green chili into a mortar or grinding chamber. Apply mechanical shear force to rupture cellular structures and liberate volatile aromatic oils.
  2. Emulsion Homogenization: Place the heavy curd matrix into a mixing vessel. Use a traditional wooden churner (*Mathu*) or rotational blender to break down lipids into a perfectly smooth liquid phase.
  3. Solvent Dilution: Slowly introduce the chilled aqueous solvent (ice water) into the dairy lipid emulsion while maintaining continuous agitation to prevent phase separation.
  4. Catalyst Inversion: Fold the extracted mint-chili compound paste, roasted cumin molecules, and crystalline mineral salts completely into the solution.
  5. Aeration & Serve: Agitate heavily for 30 seconds to generate a stable micro-foam surface gas matrix. Decant immediately into glass collection units, garnish with a single mint leaf, and consume.
