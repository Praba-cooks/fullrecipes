Pudina Buttermilk // Compound: Mint-Infused Lipid Solution

An analysis of endothermic cooling dynamics. This protocol utilizes the volatile, organic essential oils found in fresh mint leaves, bound within a fermented dairy emulsion, to induce an immediate systemic thermodynamic cooling effect.

SYNTHESIS TIME 10 MINS
REACTION TEMP 274 KELVIN
VOLUME YIELD 2x TALL BEAKERS

🧪 REAGENT COMPOSITION

🧬 CORE METHOD PROTOCOL

  1. 01 / Cellular Dissolution Apply intense mechanical shear force to the fresh mint leaves and green chili to rupture cellular walls, freeing the active volatile oils.
  2. 02 / Emulsion Homogenization Agitate the fermented curd matrix using a high-velocity rotational blender or traditional wooden churner until completely fluid.
  3. 03 / Aqueous Dilution Slowly blend the 274K chilled H₂O into the lipid base, maintaining constant velocity to prevent phase separation.
  4. 04 / Catalyst Inversion Fold in the processed mint-capsaicin compound paste, roasted cumin molecules, and crystalline mineral salts.
  5. 05 / Kinetic Aeration Froth the solution surface rapidly for 30 seconds to generate a stable gas-in-liquid micro-foam matrix. Dispense directly into glasses.
